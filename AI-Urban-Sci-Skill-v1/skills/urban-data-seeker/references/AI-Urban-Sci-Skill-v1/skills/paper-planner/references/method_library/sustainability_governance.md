## SDG_voluntary_local_review_coding

- **Paper**: Analysis of Voluntary Local Reviews reveals how cities follow up and review the SDGs (npj Urban Sustainability, 2025)
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s42949-025-00243-7
- **Domain**: sustainability_governance
- **Study design**: cross_sectional
- **Outcome type**: rate
- **Inferential target**: distributional_pattern

### Method specification
- **Method name**: Systematic content analysis coding + Total Review Score (TRS) framework
- **Hyperparameters**:
  - Sample: 151 VLR reports from 121 cities (2018-2022); sourced from UCLG, IGES VLR Lab, UNDESA, UN-HABITAT databases; 22 cities produced multiple editions
  - Coding protocol: Two authors + research assistant independently coded each report; bilingual review (local language + English); machine translation (DeepL, Google Translate) for Albanian/German/Norwegian; discrepancy resolution via discussion
  - Indicators per SDG per report:
    - Outcome-based Review Indicator (ORI) = 1 if report uses >= 1 quantitative indicator to assess city-wide SDG conditions
    - Action-based Review Indicator (ARI) = 1 if report presents >= 1 action/policy/program/project with performance data
    - Generic descriptions and civil society activities without municipal policy backing excluded
  - Total Review Score (TRS): composite score combining ORI and ARI across all 17 SDGs
  - Geographic classification: UN Geoscheme (LAC, Europe, Asia, Northern America, Africa, Oceania)
  - Income classification: World Bank (high, upper-middle, lower-middle income)
  - City size: small (< 200K), medium (200K-1M), large (1M-10M), megacity (> 10M)
- **Required validations**: Inter-coder reliability via independent coding + comparison; bilingual cross-check; exclusion criteria consistency (3 reports excluded for insufficient review content)
- **Characteristic outputs**: Per-SDG TRS by region and year; ORI vs. AR share per SDG; city-level factor analysis (resource availability, size, growth, VLR experience); international organization involvement patterns; guideline adoption trends
